# Master References

This consolidated list supports the Version 1.0 review manuscript. It is intentionally concise and should remain in review until exact edition, URL, and citation formatting are approved for final publication.

## Training, Programming, And Exercise Selection

1. American College of Sports Medicine. ACSM's Guidelines for Exercise Testing and Prescription. Wolters Kluwer, 2021. https://www.acsm.org/education-resources/books/guidelines-exercise-testing-prescription
   Used in Implementation, Training, and Tracking. Standard exercise-prescription reference; confirm edition for final layout.

2. American College of Sports Medicine. Progression Models in Resistance Training for Healthy Adults. Medicine & Science in Sports & Exercise, 2009. https://doi.org/10.1249/MSS.0b013e3181915670
   Used in Training, Exercise Library, and Tracking. Supports conservative progression, volume, intensity, and exercise selection context.

3. Haff GG, Triplett NT, editors. Essentials of Strength Training and Conditioning, 4th ed. National Strength and Conditioning Association / Human Kinetics, 2016. https://us.humankinetics.com/products/essentials-of-strength-training-and-conditioning-4th-edition-with-hkpropel-access
   Used in Training and Exercise Library. Standard strength and conditioning text; confirm edition for final layout.

## Nutrition, Hydration, And Supplements

4. Thomas DT, Erdman KA, Burke LM. Nutrition and Athletic Performance. Medicine & Science in Sports & Exercise, 2016. https://doi.org/10.1249/MSS.0000000000000852
   Used in Nutrition, Implementation, and Tracking. Joint Academy of Nutrition and Dietetics, Dietitians of Canada, and ACSM position paper.

5. Jager R, Kerksick CM, Campbell BI, et al. International Society of Sports Nutrition Position Stand: Protein and Exercise. Journal of the International Society of Sports Nutrition, 2017. https://doi.org/10.1186/s12970-017-0177-8
   Used in Nutrition and Tracking. Supports protein-distribution and athlete fueling context.

6. Kerksick CM, Arent S, Schoenfeld BJ, et al. International Society of Sports Nutrition Position Stand: Nutrient Timing. Journal of the International Society of Sports Nutrition, 2017. https://doi.org/10.1186/s12970-017-0189-4
   Used in Nutrition and Implementation. Supports practical protein/carbohydrate timing context without requiring rigid meal timing.

7. Kreider RB, Kalman DS, Antonio J, et al. International Society of Sports Nutrition Position Stand: Safety and Efficacy of Creatine Supplementation in Exercise, Sport, and Medicine. Journal of the International Society of Sports Nutrition, 2017. https://doi.org/10.1186/s12970-017-0173-z
   Used in Nutrition and Supplements. Supports conservative creatine discussion; does not replace clinician review.

8. Guest NS, VanDusseldorp TA, Nelson MT, et al. International Society of Sports Nutrition Position Stand: Caffeine and Exercise Performance. Journal of the International Society of Sports Nutrition, 2021. https://doi.org/10.1186/s12970-020-00383-4
   Used in Nutrition, Supplements, and Shift Work. Supports caffeine context and caution around sleep and individual tolerance.

9. Sawka MN, Burke LM, Eichner ER, et al. American College of Sports Medicine Position Stand: Exercise and Fluid Replacement. Medicine & Science in Sports & Exercise, 2007. https://doi.org/10.1249/mss.0b013e31802ca597
   Used in Nutrition, Hydration, and Recovery. Supports exercise hydration context; final copy should avoid individualized medical prescriptions.

## Heat, Shift Work, Sleep, And Recovery

10. National Institute for Occupational Safety and Health. Workplace Recommendations: Heat Stress. Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, 2026. https://www.cdc.gov/niosh/heat-stress/recommendations/index.html
    Used in Nutrition, Recovery, and Implementation. Current workplace heat-stress guidance for hydration, rest, acclimatization, training, and escalation context.

11. Occupational Safety and Health Administration. Heat: Working in Outdoor and Indoor Heat Environments. U.S. Department of Labor, 2026. https://www.osha.gov/heat-exposure
    Used in Recovery and Implementation. OSHA heat-exposure overview for planning, prevention, and worker-safety context.

12. Watson NF, Badr MS, Belenky G, et al. Recommended Amount of Sleep for a Healthy Adult: A Joint Consensus Statement of the AASM and Sleep Research Society. Journal of Clinical Sleep Medicine, 2015. https://doi.org/10.5664/jcsm.4758
    Used in Recovery, Shift Work, and Tracking. Supports adult sleep-duration context; not used as medical diagnosis or treatment guidance.

13. Centers for Disease Control and Prevention. About Sleep. Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, 2024. https://www.cdc.gov/sleep/about/index.html
    Used in Recovery and Tracking. Public-health sleep overview for sleep consistency and insufficient-sleep context.

14. National Institute for Occupational Safety and Health. NIOSH Training for Nurses on Shift Work and Long Work Hours. Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, 2020. https://www.cdc.gov/niosh/work-hour-training-for-nurses/longhours/default.html
    Used in Recovery, Shift Work, and Implementation. Fatigue-aware scheduling and shift-work education context; apply conservatively outside healthcare.
